Unwritten Gym Rules

Equipment & Space

  • Re‑rack your weights: Always return dumbbells, plates, and bars to their proper place.
  • No curling in the squat rack: Save the rack for squats and heavy lifts.
  • Don’t hog machines: If you’re resting, let others work in between sets.
  • Respect personal space: Don’t crowd someone mid‑set or hover too close.

Hygiene & Cleanliness

  • Wipe down equipment: Use a towel or spray after each use.
  • Bring a towel: Keep sweat off benches and mats.
  • Wear clean gym clothes: Hygiene matters in shared spaces.

Noise & Atmosphere

  • Keep grunting reasonable: Effort is fine, but don’t be disruptive.
  • Don’t drop weights unnecessarily: It’s loud and can damage equipment.
  • Limit phone use: No loud calls or blocking machines while scrolling.

Courtesy & Community

  • Offer a spot when appropriate: Help others safely complete heavy lifts.
  • Don’t interrupt mid‑set: Wait until someone finishes before asking questions.
  • Respect gym staff and rules: They keep the place safe and functional.
  • Encourage, don’t judge: Everyone’s fitness journey is different.

Beginner Gym Notes

A complete guide to your first steps in the gym. Hover to preview tips, click to lock them open, and check the glossary for common lingo.

1. Mindset & Approach

+
  • Focus on consistency over intensity — showing up matters more than crushing one workout.
  • Don’t compare yourself to advanced lifters; progress is personal.
  • Treat mistakes as learning, not failure.

2. Warm‑Up & Cool‑Down

+
  • 5–10 minutes of light cardio (bike, treadmill, rower).
  • Dynamic stretches before lifting, static stretches after.
  • Cooling down helps reduce soreness and stiffness.

3. Start with Machines

+
  • Machines guide your movement and reduce injury risk.
  • Good picks: chest press, lat pulldown, leg press, seated row.
  • Once confident, transition to free weights for natural movement.

4. Learn the Big Lifts

+
  • Compound exercises: Squat, Bench press, Deadlift, Overhead press.
  • These hit multiple muscle groups for maximum efficiency.
  • Start light, master form, then gradually add weight.

5. Plan Short Sessions

+
  • 45–60 minutes is plenty for a beginner.
  • 2–3 days per week to start, with rest days in between.
  • Focus on full-body or basic split routines.

6. Nutrition & Hydration

+
  • Drink water before, during, and after your session.
  • Eat protein + carbs within 1–2 hours post‑workout.
  • Focus on whole foods before worrying about supplements.

7. Track Progress

+
  • Write down weights, sets, and reps every session.
  • Celebrate small wins like better form or stamina.
  • Patience is key—progress is a marathon, not a sprint.

Gym Lingo 101

Reps (Repetitions)

A single completion of an exercise movement.

Sets

A group of repetitions performed before taking a rest break.

PR (Personal Record)

Your best performance in a specific lift (weight or reps).

Compound Lift

Exercises that use multiple joints and muscle groups at once.

Hypertrophy

Training specifically designed to increase muscle size.

DOMS

Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness—that ache you feel 24-48 hours later.
